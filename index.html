Café Dataset Update – Vienna 1st District

Visualizing air conditioning availability in cafés to support heat adaptation in urban spaces

Motivation

Everyone knows the situation: it’s over 30°C, you’re strolling through the city and decide to take a break in a café to cool down and escape the heat – only to realize it’s just as hot inside.

Vienna’s city center – especially the 1st district – heats up significantly in summer due to dense surfaces and a lack of greenery. Many people – especially older adults and vulnerable groups – depend on cool places to rest and recover. Yet currently, no reliable dataset exists showing which cafés offer air conditioning.

Our goal: Create an open, updated dataset that helps people identify and access cooled cafés in the city center.

Dataset Before

The original dataset from OpenStreetMap contained information on 139 cafés in Vienna’s 1st district. However, the data was incomplete:

  • Only 6 cafés (~4%) had information about air conditioning.
  • 26% had no opening hours listed.
  • 32% had no street name.
  • 53% were missing a website.
  • 96% had no AC information.

Dataset After

After updating and verifying each café manually, the dataset now includes 120 valid cafés. We removed duplicates and permanently closed locations, and added missing attributes through web search.

  • 69 cafés with air conditioning (~58%)
  • 43 cafés without air conditioning (~36%)
  • 8 cafés with no information (~7%)

Limitations

Some limitations still apply to the dataset. Despite best efforts, a few entries could not be verified or had to be excluded:

  • 19 cafés were removed due to duplication or closure
  • 12 values across the dataset could not be updated (8x AC info, 3x website, 1x opening hours)
  • We successfully adjusted 287 incorrect or missing values overall

Use Case

This enriched dataset can support various applications:

  • OpenStreetMap Contribution: Reintegration of verified data helps improve OSM quality.
  • Public Heat Resilience: Urban planning can use the data to guide vulnerable groups to cooled spaces.
  • Smart City Apps: Integration into WienBot or similar platforms enables real-time user guidance.
  • Urban Analytics: The data supports spatial analysis of cooling infrastructure and social equity.
